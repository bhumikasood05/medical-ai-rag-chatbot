Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, usually caused by an infection or illness.

A normal body temperature is around 98.6°F (37°C). A fever is generally considered when the body temperature is 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.

Common symptoms of fever include:
- Chills and shivering
- Sweating
- Headache
- Muscle aches
- Loss of appetite
- Weakness and fatigue
- Dehydration

Common causes of fever include:
- Viral infections such as the common cold and flu
- Bacterial infections
- Food poisoning
- Heat exhaustion
- Inflammatory conditions

Self-care measures for mild fever:
- Drink plenty of fluids.
- Get adequate rest.
- Wear lightweight clothing.
- Take medications only as advised by a healthcare professional.

Seek medical attention if:
- Fever lasts for more than three days.
- Body temperature rises above 103°F (39.4°C).
- There is difficulty breathing, chest pain, severe headache, confusion, seizures, or persistent vomiting.
- Infants younger than three months develop a fever.
