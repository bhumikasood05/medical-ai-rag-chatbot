Hypertension, also called high blood pressure, occurs when the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high.

It is often called a "silent killer" because many people do not experience symptoms.

Risk factors include obesity, stress, excessive salt intake, smoking, lack of exercise, and family history.

Managing hypertension includes regular exercise, reducing salt intake, maintaining a healthy weight, and taking prescribed medications.

Seek medical advice for diagnosis and treatment.
