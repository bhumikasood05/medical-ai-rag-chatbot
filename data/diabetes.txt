Diabetes is a chronic condition in which the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) levels.

There are three main types of diabetes:
- Type 1 Diabetes: The immune system attacks insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.
- Type 2 Diabetes: The body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin.
- Gestational Diabetes: Develops during pregnancy and usually disappears after childbirth.

Common symptoms of diabetes include:
- Frequent urination
- Increased thirst
- Increased hunger
- Fatigue
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds
- Unexplained weight loss
- Tingling or numbness in hands and feet

Risk factors for Type 2 diabetes include:
- Family history of diabetes
- Obesity or overweight
- Physical inactivity
- High blood pressure
- Increasing age
- Unhealthy eating habits

Ways to manage diabetes:
- Follow a balanced and healthy diet.
- Exercise regularly.
- Monitor blood sugar levels.
- Take medications or insulin as prescribed by a doctor.
- Attend regular health check-ups.

Seek medical advice if you experience symptoms of diabetes or have concerns about your blood sugar levels.
